Nature

Morning Light

March 2025

The sun arrives not like a conqueror but like a question — soft at the curtain's edge, asking if we're ready. I am never ready. I am always standing in last night's clothes, holding yesterday in both hands. But the light comes anyway. That is what light does. It does not ask permission. It does not wait for you to stop grieving. It arrives. It fills the room. It says: here you are, still here.

Poet's Note

This poem was written on a January morning when I couldn't decide whether to be sad or grateful. The light made the decision for me.
